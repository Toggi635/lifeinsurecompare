Indexed Universal Life Insurance: Pros and Cons Explained

1. What Is Indexed Universal Life Insurance?

Indexed universal life (IUL) insurance offers cash value growth linked to the performance of a stock market index with downside protection. Unlike variable life, IUL includes a guaranteed minimum crediting rate.

2. How IUL Crediting Strategies Work

IUL policies credit interest using strategies like annual point-to-point and monthly average. Each strategy has a cap rate, participation rate, and floor. Current cap rates range from 7% to 12%.

Important: IUL caps and participation rates can change annually.

3. Pros of Indexed Universal Life

The primary advantage is downside protection with upside potential. Your cash value never decreases due to market losses. IUL also offers flexible premiums and tax-deferred growth.

4. Cons and Considerations

IUL policies are complex with many moving parts. Cap rates can change annually. Policy expenses can significantly impact cash value accumulation.

5. Is IUL Right for Your Financial Plan?

IUL is best for those wanting permanent life insurance with growth potential exceeding whole life, without direct market risk. It should not be purchased solely as an investment.
