1. Life Insurance as an Investment

Certain permanent policies accumulate cash value that can serve as a financial asset. Understanding how cash value works is essential before considering life insurance as an investment.

2. How Cash Value Accumulates

A portion of each premium goes toward cash value that grows tax-deferred. In early years, cash value grows slowly due to front-loaded fees.

3. Comparing Cash Value Policy Types

Whole life offers guaranteed growth with dividends. Universal life growth based on crediting rates. IUL ties growth to market indices with downside protection.

Important: Cash values are typically illiquid in early years due to surrender charges.

Advertisement

4. Tax Advantages

Cash value grows tax-deferred. Policy loans are tax-free if the policy stays in force. Death benefits are income tax-free to beneficiaries.

5. Important Considerations

Cash value life insurance should not be your primary investment. Max out 401(k) and IRA first. Returns are typically conservative at 4-6%.
