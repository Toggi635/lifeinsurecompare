Life Insurance Medical Exam: What to Expect and How to Prepare

1. What to Expect from a Life Insurance Medical Exam

Many life insurance policies require a medical exam as part of the underwriting process. In 2026, the exam is typically quick, painless, and can be done at your home or office.

2. What the Exam Tests

The exam includes blood and urine tests checking for cholesterol, blood sugar, nicotine, HIV, and other markers. Blood pressure, height, weight, and pulse are measured. Some insurers also check cognitive function for older applicants.

3. How to Prepare

Fast for 8-12 hours before the exam (water is fine). Avoid alcohol for 24-48 hours. Avoid exercise for 12 hours. Get a good night's sleep. Stay hydrated. Avoid caffeine and nicotine on exam day.

Important: Being well-rested and hydrated can lower blood pressure readings and improve test results, potentially saving you money on premiums.

4. Tips for Best Results

Schedule the exam in the morning when blood pressure is naturally lower. Wear comfortable clothing. Bring a list of medications. Be honest about your health history.

5. After the Exam

Results typically take 2-4 weeks. You may qualify for better rates than applied for if results are favorable. If results show issues, ask about alternative rate classes.
