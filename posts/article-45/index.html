First-to-Die Life Insurance: What It Is and Who Needs It

1. What Is First-to-Die Life Insurance?

First-to-die life insurance covers two people and pays the death benefit when the first insured dies. It is less common than second-to-die but useful for specific situations.

2. How First-to-Die Policies Work

The policy covers two individuals. Premiums are based on both ages and health. When the first person dies, the death benefit is paid to the surviving insured or other named beneficiary. Coverage for the second person typically ends.

3. Common Uses for First-to-Die Policies

Couples who need coverage for mortgage protection or income replacement. Business partners who need buy-sell funding. Co-borrowers on joint loans.

Note: First-to-die policies are less common than buying two separate term policies, which often provide more flexibility.

Advertisement

4. Pros and Cons

Pros include lower combined premium compared to two individual policies. Cons include complexity and that coverage ends for the survivor after the first death.

5. First-to-Die vs Separate Policies

For most couples, buying separate term policies provides more flexibility and better value. First-to-die can make sense when only one policy is needed to cover a specific joint obligation.
