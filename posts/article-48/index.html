1. Life Insurance Beneficiary Basics

Choosing the right beneficiaries for your life insurance policy is crucial. Understanding the rules for primary, contingent, and tertiary beneficiaries ensures your death benefit goes to the right people.

2. Primary vs Contingent Beneficiaries

A primary beneficiary is first in line to receive the death benefit. A contingent beneficiary receives the benefit if the primary beneficiary dies before you. You can name multiple beneficiaries and specify percentages.

3. Revocable vs Irrevocable Beneficiaries

A revocable beneficiary can be changed at any time without their consent. An irrevocable beneficiary cannot be changed without their written consent. Irrevocable designations are common in divorce settlements.

Important: If you name an irrevocable beneficiary, you lose the ability to change your policy's beneficiary designation without their permission.

4. Common Beneficiary Mistakes

Naming minors as direct beneficiaries (they cannot receive death benefits directly). Naming your estate (subjects proceeds to probate). Not updating beneficiaries after divorce or death. Not naming contingent beneficiaries.

5. Best Practices

Name both primary and contingent beneficiaries. Review beneficiary designations annually. Update after major life events. Consider naming a trust for minor children. Be specific about identification.
