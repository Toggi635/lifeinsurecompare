1. Life Insurance After Age 70: What You Need to Know

Securing life insurance after age 70 is more challenging than at younger ages, but several insurers specialize in coverage for older applicants. In 2026, seniors over 70 have access to guaranteed issue policies, simplified issue whole life, and small term life policies.

The primary reasons seniors over 70 purchase life insurance include covering funeral and burial expenses, leaving a legacy, and providing for a surviving spouse.

2. Best Life Insurance Companies for Seniors Over 70

Colonial Penn leads our ranking for seniors over 70 with its guaranteed acceptance whole life policies that require no medical exam or health questions. Mutual of Omaha offers simplified issue whole life up to age 80. AARP (New York Life) provides guaranteed issue coverage for members aged 50-80.

3. Understanding Graded Death Benefits

Most guaranteed issue life insurance policies include a graded death benefit provision. If the insured dies within the first 2 to 3 years, beneficiaries receive only the premiums paid plus interest rather than the full death benefit.

The full death benefit becomes effective after the graded period ends. This provision allows insurers to offer coverage without medical underwriting.

5. Alternatives to Traditional Life Insurance for Seniors

For seniors over 70 who find traditional life insurance too expensive, alternatives exist. Pre-need funeral insurance allows you to pre-pay for funeral services. Accidental death insurance provides coverage for accident-related deaths at lower rates.

Compare total premiums versus death benefit when evaluating any policy.
