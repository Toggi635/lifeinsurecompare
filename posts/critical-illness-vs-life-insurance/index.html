Frequently Asked Questions

What does critical illness insurance cover?

Pays a lump sum if you are diagnosed with a covered condition like cancer, heart attack, or stroke.

How is critical illness different from life insurance?

Critical illness pays you while you are alive; life insurance pays your beneficiaries after death.

Can critical illness replace life insurance?

No, they serve different purposes. Critical illness covers survival costs; life insurance protects dependents.

Is critical illness insurance worth it?

It can be valuable if you lack sufficient savings to cover deductibles, treatment costs, and lost income.

Should I buy both?

Yes, they complement each other. Life insurance for your family, critical illness for your survival needs.
