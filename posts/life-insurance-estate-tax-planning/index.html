1. Estate Taxes and Life Insurance

Life Insurance for Estate Tax Planning: Strategies for Wealth Transfer is an important topic for anyone considering their financial future. This section explores key aspects of estate taxes and life insurance and how it applies to your specific situation. Understanding these concepts will help you make informed decisions about your life insurance coverage.

When evaluating your options, consider factors such as your budget, long-term goals, and family circumstances. Each person's situation is unique, and the right choice depends on a careful assessment of your individual needs and priorities.

Insurance professionals recommend reviewing your coverage periodically to ensure it continues to meet your needs. Life changes such as marriage, children, career changes, and retirement all affect the type and amount of coverage you should carry.

By taking the time to understand your options and working with a licensed insurance professional, you can secure the right life insurance policy that provides peace of mind and financial protection for those who depend on you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does life insurance help with estate taxes?

Life insurance provides immediate, tax-free cash to pay estate taxes, preventing forced asset sales.

What is the current federal estate tax exemption?

The exemption is .61 million per individual (2026). Amounts above this are taxed at 40%.

Which states have estate taxes?

About 12 states and DC have estate or inheritance taxes with lower exemptions than federal.

What is an ILIT and why use one?

An Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust removes the policy from your estate, keeping proceeds estate-tax-free.

Can I gift premiums to an ILIT?

Yes, premium gifts to an ILIT qualify for the annual gift tax exclusion (,000 per year in 2026).
