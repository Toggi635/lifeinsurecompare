Frequently Asked Questions

How does second-to-die life insurance work?

The policy pays the death benefit after both insureds have died, not after the first death.

What is the main advantage of second-to-die?

Premiums are lower than two individual policies because the payout occurs later.

Is second-to-die only for estate taxes?

No, it is also used for charitable giving, business succession, and wealth transfer.

Who owns the policy?

Typically an Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT) owns the policy to keep proceeds out of the estate.

Can unmarried couples buy second-to-die?

Yes, any two people with insurable interest can purchase a survivorship policy.
