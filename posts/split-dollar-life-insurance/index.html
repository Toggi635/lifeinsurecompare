Frequently Asked Questions

How does split-dollar life insurance work?

The employer and employee split the premium and share the death benefit and cash value according to a written agreement.

What are the two main types?

Economic benefit regime (employer pays premium, employee pays imputed income) and loan regime (employer loans premium to employee).

What are the tax implications for employees?

Under the economic benefit regime, the employee reports the value of the economic benefit as income.

Is split-dollar still commonly used?

Less common after 2003 IRS regulations but still used for key executive benefits.

Who should consider split-dollar?

Businesses looking to provide life insurance benefits to key executives without current tax deduction limitations.
